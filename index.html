Greater Roadrunner

"Though I was born in Amarillo, TX, my earlier memories are from the handful of years in which my family lived in Albuquerque. I have vague and hazy recollections of seeing these birds. But on a family vacation (as an adult) in 2018, my heart leapt when I saw a greater roadrunner living up to its name. The bird was running along a service street in the middle of the scrub and sand of New Mexico. We were traveling to Glorietta and I got to see one! It was pretty awesome."

Pileated Woodpecker

"Woodpeckers have always fascinated me. The physics of how they can hammer their beaks into solid wood without concussing themselves is so cool. Our first home was by a beauitful park on the Meramec. They're about crow-sized (17" or so) and they sound like monkeys when they call out. I hope you get to see one! They're not too common in Missouri."

Barn Owl

"This isn't just my favorite bird. It's my favorite animal. Period. Sadly, I have never seen one in the wild. They have a terrible call (Google it), but I think the heart-shaped face is stunning. Silent killers, they mostly eat rodents. Maybe the fact that there aren't that many barns left in Missouri is a factor in their not being very common these days."

Eurasian Tree Sparrow

"Now these little guys really are fun! In the spring of 1870, a shipment of birds from Germany was released into St. Louis. The hope was that the sight of familiar species would be a welcome sight to European immigrants. And in that shipment were a dozen Eurasian Tree Sparrows! In Missouri, they're really only found in St. Louis (you'll also see them across the river in Illinois). The more aggressive House Sparrow keep their populations from gaining, but they're easily distinguished by the black spot on their cheeks."

Scarlet Tanager

"I've only seen this bird once. It was at Lone Elk Park. With their unmistakable black and scarlet plumage (the females are olive-yellow), these birds are usually hard to see. They spend a lot of time in the treetops, but the one I saw in the park was perched on a dead tree in the middle of a clearing. Lovely."
